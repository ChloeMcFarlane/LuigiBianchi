Artist Statement
I am an actor who asks questions. I seek truth in my work and meaning in the stories I tell. As a Puerto Rican, Dominican, and Italian artist, culture is not something I reference—it’s something I carry. It shapes how I speak, how I see the world, and how I approach every character. I am drawn to stories that challenge me personally and challenge the narratives around my communities, especially in how they reveal our complexity, beauty, and humanity.

I am based between Boston and New York, with plans to expand toward Los Angeles in the future. Performance, for me, is a process of learning. Watching a character move through struggle, contradiction, and transformation teaches me something about myself each time. More importantly, I hope that work can reflect something back to young men of color who are still searching for that same sense of light and possibility.

After training at Boston University and Boston Arts Academy, I can say I’ve been in this work most of my life—but I also know I am still at the beginning. That ongoing process of discovery is what excites me most. Outside of acting, I can be found rock climbing, training in the gym, making music, thrifting and building fits, and finding good food wherever I can.
